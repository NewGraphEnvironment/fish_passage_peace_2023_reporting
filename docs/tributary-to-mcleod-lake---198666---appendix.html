Restoring Fish Passage in the Peace Region - 2023

Tributary to McLeod Lake - 198666 - Appendix

Site Location

PSCIS crossing 198666 is located on Tributary to McLeod Lake. This site is located on Highway 97, approximately 150m upstream from McLeod Lake. The stream crossing is located between McLeod Lake Store and the Tse’Khene food and fuel entrance.


Background

Upstream of crossing 198666, rainbow trout have previously been recorded (Norris 2022).

A summary of habitat modelling outputs is presented in Table 3.1. A map of the watershed is provided in map attachment 093J.123.

Stream Characteristics at Crossing

At the time of the survey, PSCIS crossing 198666 was un-embedded, non-backwatered and ranked as a barrier to upstream fish passage according to the provincial protocol (MoE 2011) (Table 3.2). There was high flow through the culvert at the time of survey. A deep outlet pool (2m) and a large outlet drop (2m) indicate that the culvert may be undersized. Water temperature was 12.7\(^\circ\)C, pH was 7.9 and conductivity was 410uS/cm.

Stream Characteristics Downstream

The stream was surveyed downstream from crossing 198666 for 250m (Figures 3.1 - 3.2). The average channel width was 2.8m, the average wetted width was 1.9m, and the average gradient was 2.7%.Total cover amount was rated as moderate with overhanging vegetation dominant. Cover was also present as undercut banks.The dominant substrate was gravels with cobbles sub-dominant. There was an abundance of large woody debris that created deep pools, diverted water flow, and created natural steps. The habitat was rated as NA value for salmonid rearing.

Stream Characteristics Upstream

The stream was surveyed upstream from crossing 198666 for 220m (Figures 3.3 - 3.6). Total cover amount was rated as moderate with large woody debris dominant. Cover was also present as small woody debris, undercut banks, overhanging vegetation, and instream vegetation.The dominant substrate was fines with fines sub-dominant.The average channel width was 3.4m, the average wetted width was 2.5m, and the average gradient was 1.5%. This stream has the potential to be fish bearing but the gradient is steep. The bank full depth is very high, with a measured average of 0.8m. There was an abundance of large woody debris that provided functionality to the stream habitat by diverting flow and providing cover. The habitat was rated as NA value as an important migration corridor containing suitable rearing habitat.


Structure Remediation and Cost Estimate

Should restoration/maintenance activities proceed, replacement of PSCIS crossing 198666 with a bridge (4.5m span) is recommended. The cost of the work is estimated at $1,500,000 for a cost benefit of 3120 linear m/$1000 and 4680 m2/$1000.


Conclusion

There was 4.7km of habitat modelled upstream of crossing 198666 with areas surveyed rated as NA value for salmonid rearing and spawning. 198666 was ranked as a NA priority for proceeding to design for replacement. Electrofishing upstream and downstream of the culvert is recommended to provide insight into fish community composition and density which may justify a higher priority for site remediation.
